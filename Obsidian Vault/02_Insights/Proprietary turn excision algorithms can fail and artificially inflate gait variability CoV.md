Proprietary turn excision algorithms can fail silently and produce misleadingly elevated CoV values for gait variability, independent of the degree of gait dysfunction present.

Evidence:
[[kroneberg2019LessMore]] observed instances where the Mobility Lab turn excision algorithm failed, resulting in CoV values that were spuriously high. Critically, this occurred across both healthy older adults and movement disorder groups, suggesting it is not a consequence of pathological gait but an algorithmic limitation. See [[Walking turns elevate CoV of spatiotemporal parameters when not excluded from gait analysis]] for the related finding about turn contamination more broadly.
