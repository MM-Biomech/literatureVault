Inclusion of turning strides in gait variability analysis inflates CoV of stride length and stride time and widens confidence margins, even when turns are separated by straight walking segments.

Evidence:
Kroneberg et al. found that strides adjacent to 180° turns systematically elevated CoV values across all groups. Applying a more generous turn exclusion window (removing one additional stride on each side beyond the manufacturer's algorithm) reduced group mean CoV values and partially improved ICC across walking segments. This indicates that turn contamination is a methodological confound rather than a genuine variability signal.
