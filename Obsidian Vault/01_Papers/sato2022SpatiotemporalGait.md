Purpose / Hypothesis:

- (p. 2) examine differences in spatiotemporal gait in MS and between MS subtypes during the timed 25-ft-walk test (T25FW)
- (p. 2) investigate (1) spatiotemporal differences, (2) spatiotemporal intra-participant variability differences between MS subtypes and controls at preferred and fast speed T25FW, and (3) differences in modulation of spatiotemporal gait parameters between preferred and fast speed
- (p. 2) examined spatiotemporal kinematics that are less commonly reported in the literature, such as leg circumduction, peak foot clearance, and ankle pitch at heel strike and toe-off
- (p. 2) hypothesized that PwMS would demonstrate a 'cautious' gait pattern: slower speed, longer step duration, shorter swing time, longer double support time, shorter stride length, less foot clearance, and more circumduction; PMS would walk slower with longer stride lengths and longer double support times compared to RRMS

Participant Demographics:

- (p. 2) relapse-remitting, secondary progressive, and primary progressive MS subtypes — see demographics table above

Methods Details:

- (p. 2) six inertial sensors (Opal, Version 2, APDM Wearable Technologies Inc., Portland, OR, USA) equipped with a triaxial accelerometer and gyroscope on the sternum, lumbar spine, left and right wrists, and at the dorsum of each foot
- (p. 2) preferred and fast-as-possible T25FW speeds, one trial each
- (p. 2) speed (m/s), step duration (s), swing time (% stride time), double support time (% stride time), and stride length (m); ankle pitch angles (degrees) at heel-strike and toe-off; foot clearance (cm); circumduction (cm — maximum lateral deviation of the foot during swing)
- (p. 2) measures were averaged for all gait cycles for each participant and across left and right limbs
- (p. 2) average ± SD of gait cycles: preferred T25FW 5 ± 1.43; fast T25FW 4 ± 1.38 — CoV metrics likely unreliable
- (p. 2) group mean differences examined using one-way ANOVA with unequal variances (Welch's test), post hoc pairwise t-tests with 95% CI (Satterthwaite's method)
- (p. 3) effect sizes calculated for pairwise comparisons allowing unequal variance
- (p. 3) one-way t-test on the within-subject difference used to examine preferred vs. fast T25FW modulation within each group

## Results
%% begin results %%
- (p. 3) Swing time and stride length were longer, while double support time was shorter in controls than both MS subtypes
- (p. 3) Ankle pitch at heel strike and toe-off were greater in controls than both MS subtypes
- (p. 3) No significant group differences in step duration, foot clearance, or circumduction (all p > 0.05)
- (p. 3) At fast T25FW: faster speed in controls than RRMS and PMS, with longer stride length and swing time and shorter double support time and step duration
- (p. 3) No significant group differences in foot clearance or circumduction at fast speed
- (p. 3) No significant differences in spatiotemporal gait parameters between MS subtypes at either speed
- (p. 8) Modulation in foot clearance was significantly different between groups: greater difference between controls and RRMS (ES = 0.886) than between controls and PMS (ES = 0.460)
%% end results %%

## Key Insights Extracted
%% begin key-insights %%
- [[People with multiple sclerosis likely attempt to increase dynamic stability by increasing the time spend in double support and reducing their stride lengths and stride time]]
- [[Spatiotemporal metrics differ between controls and people with multiple sclerosis during the T25FW at preferred and fast walking speeds]]
- [[Spatiotemporal metrics did not differ between people with multiple sclerosis with relapse remitting and progressive subtypes during the T25FW]]
- [[Ankle pitch at heel strike and toe off is reduced in people with multiple sclerosis compared to controls during a T25FW at both preferred and fast walking speeds]]
- [[Foot clearance is difficult for people with multiple sclerosis to modulate at higher walking speeds during the T25FW]]
- [[Ankle pitch is well modulated for people with multiple sclerosis at higher walking speeds during the T25FW despite kinematic impairment]]
- [[Sato et al found no differences in foot clearance or circumduction between controls and people with multiple sclerosis]]

- (p. 3) PwMS walk with shorter stride length and swing time and longer double support and stride time compared to controls — strategies likely adopted to increase dynamic stability
- (p. 4) In ambulatory PwMS, spatiotemporal gait parameters were different from controls, but group differences between RRMS and PMS were not significant at both preferred and fast speeds.
- (p. 6) No significant differences in circumduction were observed, consistent with the foot clearance results.
- (p. 6) Ankle pitch at HS was significantly greater in controls than both MS groups at preferred and fast speeds. At toe-off, significantly lower pitch angle in both MS groups vs. controls during fast walking, with a similar but smaller effect at preferred speed.
- (p. 8) Modulation in foot clearance was significantly different between groups, with greater difference between controls and RRMS (ES = 0.886) than between controls and PMS (ES = 0.460)
