## Study Context
%% begin study-context %%
Population:
- [[Healthy Adults]]
- [[Healthy Older Adults]]
-[[Parkinson's Disease]]] - Early stage [[Hoehn and Yahr]] stage ≤ 2

Sample Size:
 - 16 Healthy used for validation 7 men and 9 women, age: 31.13 ± 4.4 years, height: 167.75 ± 7.06 cm, weight: 63.06 ± 14.89 kg)
 - 30 from each group for Cross sectional design
Activity:
- [[Straight-line walking]]
- [[Dual-task Walking]] - Subtraction

Methods:
- [[Smart insole]] - Gilon 40 Hz, four pressure sensors (force-sensitive resistors, FSR) positioned at the toe, medial forefoot, lateral forefoot, and heel; one triaxial accelerometer
- [[Optical motion capture]] - Vicon, 100 Hz, 10 cameras
- [[Force plate]] - 100 Hz, 2 plates

Statistics:
- [[Bland-Altman]]
- [[ICC]] - 2,1 absolute
- [[ANOVA]]
- [[Pearson Correlation]]
Purpose / Hypothesis:

- characterize plantar pressure alterations in patients with early-stage PD (EPD) during dual-task walking using a commercially available sensor-based insole system
- compared gait metrics, including spatiotemporal parameters and plantar pressure metrics, measured during single- and dual-task walking in healthy young adults, older adults, and patients with EPD

Methods Details:

- (p. 2) sensor data sampled at 40 Hz
- (p. 2) Spatiotemporal gait parameters including velocity, cadence, stride length, stride time, and swing ratio
- (p. 3) VICON system was equipped with ten cameras and two separate force plates (60 × 50 cm) for each foot, with a sampling frequency of 100 Hz.
- (p. 3) self-selected comfortable walking speed along a designated walkway within the VICON capture volume.
- (p. 3) Criterion validity was evaluated by comparing the spatiotemporal parameters from GDCA-MD® with those obtained from the VICON system. The interclass correlation coefficient (ICC; 2,1) was calculated using a two-way random model with absolute agreement. A 95% confidence interval was applied. Bland–Altman plots were used to assess bias and potential proportional errors.
- (p. 3) dual-task gait analysis performed both a single-motor task and a cognitive dual-task while walking along a flat, obstacle-free hallway wearing instrumented insoles
- (p. 3) performed the 10 MWT again under a cognitive dual-task condition (10 MWTCog), which involved serially subtracting three from a randomly chosen number between 100 and 999, as instructed by the evaluator. The participants were told to continue subtracting without correction even if errors occurred
- (p. 3) relative pressure measured using the FSR sensor was converted into absolute values (kPa) using the following formula [28], [29]:  Pressure kPa = 10.7835 + 0.0587 × FSR + 0.0004 × FSR2
- (p. 3) Pearson’s correlations were used to assess relationships among primary variables

## Results
%% begin results %%
- (p. 4) Velocity and stride length decreased across the groups progressing from Healthy Young (HY) to Healthy Old (HO) and Early Parkinson's Disease (EPD).
- (p. 4) The EPD group demonstrated significantly lower peak pressures, particularly in the toe, medial forefoot, and heel regions (all p < 0.001), with evident reductions relative to the HO group
- (p. 4) CV of the peak pressure in the medial forefoot was significantly lower in the EPD group than in the HO group
- (p. 4) heel CV was significantly increased compared to that in the HY group
- (p. 4) HY group, toe pressure was significantly correlated with cadence and stride time. In HO group, medial forefoot and heel pressures showed significant associations with both cadence and stride time. In EPD group, heel pressure was correlated with stride time.
%% end results %%
